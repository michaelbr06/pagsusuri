Examination of Conscience

About Pagsusuri.com


What’s this?

This is an Examination of Conscience guide for Catholics preparing for Confession. It’s based on the Two Commandments Christ (Mt. 22:37-39).


Why was this website made?

The idea of creating this website happened when I was in queue for Confession in the Antipolo Cathedral. A priest went out of this box and asked the waiting penitents if they had already examined their conscience. Perhaps he had kept on asking follow-up questions, and the line was just getting longer and wanted to cater everyone within the time frame.

This could be solved by two things:

  • having more priests available and/or
  • penitents have a good examination of conscience

This website aims to help people in their examination of conscience with the use of their phones.


How to use this website?

  1. Go to a quiet place where you can reflect on your past actions until now without being interrupted. This could be in a church, your bedroom, or your study room.
  2. Go through each of the questions. If it’s not applicable to you or if it’s something that you haven’t done—skip it. Otherwise, check it. Checked items are the sins you’ve done that you wish to confess to the priest.
  3. If you have sins that are not on the list, you can write that down or mention them after reading the list.
  4. Click on See List for a summary of checked items in bullets.
  5. If you’re already familiar with how Confession works (what to do and say), you can simply refer to the list.
  6. Otherwise, you can continue on with the Confession Guide.

Some reminders

Do not withold any sins, no matter how shameful or humiliating it is.

“…there is no sin that God’s mercy cannot reach and wipe away when it finds a repentant heart seeking to be reconciled...”

—Pope Francis, Apostolic Letter (Misericordia et misera), 2016


Wait. Is this safe?

This website is designed to serverless.
Meaning that your data isn’t saved.
You don’t even need an account to use it. To reduce the risk of exposure, the app resets when you refresh the page.
